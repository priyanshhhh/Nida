how it began

we met in a game.
we were never supposed to.

Free Fire — Dynamic Duo

It started the way things you can't explain always do — quietly, accidentally, inside a game where people go to escape their real lives. I found her there. Or maybe she found me. Or maybe neither of us found anything, and the universe just put us in the same lobby and said — figure it out.

We became a Dynamic Duo. That's what the game calls it when two people keep choosing each other, keep surviving together, keep showing up. It felt like a sign. It probably was. It just wasn't the kind of sign I thought it was.

She unfriended me. I don't know why. I never asked — or maybe I did and the answer didn't matter because the next time she added me back, I forgot I was supposed to be done. That's the thing about her. Every time felt like the first time. Every time I thought — this time it stays.

0 unfriends
in 6 months.
and every time,
I came back.

Six months. Six times. Six versions of an ending that never actually ended. The Dynamic Duo badge broke every time too — that little icon the game gives you when you play together enough. It would reset. We'd rebuild it. It would reset again.

I used to think the game was glitching. Now I think it just knew something I didn't.

She was the one person I didn't have to try around. No anxiety. No silence that needed filling. Just her — being her — and somehow that was enough to make me forget I was afraid of everything.

So I learned to love you in pieces —
and somehow that made you feel even more infinite.

  • her glances — the ones that were only ever for me

  • her voice — a sound I still hear in quiet rooms
  • her teeth when she laughed too hard to care
  • her style — effortless and entirely her
